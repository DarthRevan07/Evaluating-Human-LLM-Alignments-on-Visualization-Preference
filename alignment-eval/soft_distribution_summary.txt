  Unlike hard-label metrics that treat each model response as a
  point-mass (delta) distribution, these metrics compare the full
  probability distribution from k repeated model runs against the
  human annotator vote distribution.

  Column descriptions:
    JSD      Mean Jensen-Shannon Divergence (0 = identical, 1 = maximally different)
    Cosine   Mean cosine similarity of probability vectors (1 = identical direction)
    TV Dist  Mean total variation distance (0 = identical, 1 = disjoint support)
    Overlap  Mean distribution overlap Σ min(h_i, m_i)  (1 = identical, 0 = disjoint)
    CE       Mean cross-entropy CE(human, model) in bits (lower = model fits human better)
    Pearson  Pearson's r between human margin (h_c1−h_c2)/N and model margin across pairs
    τ_b      Kendall's Tau-b between human and model soft margins (rank concordance)
    Ent_r    Pearson's r between Hnorm(human) and Hnorm(model) entropy vectors
    Ent_cos  Cosine similarity between Hnorm(human) and Hnorm(model) entropy vectors

  Interpretation notes:
    Lower JSD / TV and higher Cosine / Overlap / Pearson / τ_b → better alignment.
    Pearson r = 1 → model margin perfectly linearly tracks human margin.
    τ_b = 1       → model margin perfectly rank-concordant with human margin.
